Quantum Servant

Exploring Determinism and Life

Dan wakes up early morning with bags under his eyes.

Life seems to fade by. He can vaguely remember getting on the bus, but it’s not more than a shallow memory; almost like he teleported to the coffee shop.

A fellow worker asks in an uplifting manner how he’s doing. A bit startled, Dan answers:

  • “I’m fine, just didn’t had much to sleep last night.''

In a mind numbing cycle of coffee after coffee, he isn’t even thinking anymore. Just wants to finish his job to return home.

  • “See you Dan, till tomorrow”

He couldn’t answer without slightly grinning, as he knew there wouldn’t be a tomorrow.

  • “See you Ann, till another day”

Back home, all doubt seemed to fade. For once, the hole he felt inside of him was filled.

He puts the gun in his mouth.

The revolver clicks, and for an instant (or at least for him it felt like an instant), Dan watched himself from outside his own head. He saw his mom finding his body. The sounds coming out of her no son should hear.

He felt someone (or rather something) holding his hand and dragging him to his funeral, following in a flash the lives of everyone he touched, ending with the last time his name was whispered.

The gun somehow failed. Nothing he saw had come. Had he dreamt all that?

Trying desperately to make sense of what had just happened, he could only stop and cry.

Was he given another chance? Did all of that really happen?

At that point things weren’t much better for him. If anything, the depression was even worse; knowing that his sudden death would affect people he wasn’t even close with. Only then had he understood how selfish it was of him to give up.

As life is, it goes on. Same old, same old, day by day seems to fade away.

A random thursday, no more than 2 weeks after the incident, his shift had ended. Walking through the city, anxious to get back home, he spots a young woman walking in the middle of the street - oblivious to the cars passing by.

He sees a bus about to make a right turn into her street. She’s in his blind spot.

Without much thought or care, he rushes to the street to push her - only to be hit himself by the bus.

A quick flash, and he was again outside his body. Everything seemed to pass through his eyes so quickly, ending with the last time his name was said. The hand brought him back home.

Suddenly, he wakes back at his bathroom - with a gun in his hand.

Completely lost and without direction, he panicked.
Without knowing exactly how was he alive, he could only stop and wonder while completely static - his face in the mirror, the same as supposedly 2 weeks ago.

Getting out of this stasis state, he went to check his phone. It was indeed the day he had attempted; or rather, successfully, killed himself.


The only thing left to do was wait. Wait until the day that silly girl walked on the wrong side of the street.

And wait he did. The days seemed somewhat more interesting, now that he was reliving them. Finding new ways to fuck with people instantly became his hobby; he didn’t quite remember as much as he wanted to, but barely enough to toy with everyone.

The day had come. He remembered the time it happened perfectly, and made sure to be in the same place at the same time, but, this time, no girl. Confused, he started looking around trying to find her, only to see her sitting in a bench waiting for the same bus.

Somehow, she wasn’t in danger. Something was different, but he couldn’t grasp what.

Without much more to do or say - heck, what would he say to the girl? I died to save you? He’d look insane - he got in a bus going home. Not very far into the way, 2 suspicious looking guys entered the bus. “It’s a robbery! Everyone purses out, give me your phones”. Both armed going through the corridor, when an old man decided not to comply with their call.

  • “Old fella just give it or I’ll blow a hole in your head”

The old man didn’t budge. The men rapidly got annoyed and started beating the old chap.

Dan didn’t want to interfere, but more than ever he wasn’t afraid of death. He could throw himself at both men, and if his past is any indication he would just wake up in his bathroom again.

Without much thinking he jumped them. He was quickly shot in the chest, but managed to grab a gun from one of the aggressors. He shot one in the head, who died immediately, while the other managed to run.

Dan was hemorrhaging, and rapidly fell to the ground. In his last moments he tried to console everyone “I’m alright; I did what had to be done, don’t worry”. He wasn’t sure he had done the right thing; and for a moment he wasn’t even sure he would actually come back. Maybe he was just a bit insane, and now he had actually gotten himself killed.

As he laid there dying, he saw a figure by his side. It’s form almost inexplicable; it looked almost as if it sucked light into itself - probably how a black hole looks from up close.

It took his hand and led him on.

Almost conscious of himself, Dan tried asking the being for what was happening to him. The entity spoke thus:

  • I’m ashamed I did it but it had to be done. More than ever I needed the help.
  • Did what? How exactly am I helping?
  • I can’t say much or I risk ruining it all. You’ll have to solve this riddle with time. One day I hope we can talk openly, but not today.
  • ...What?

In a blink of his past life he was back in his bathroom, gun in his hand. A little bit scared, but this time with an almost somber mood he was ready to do things again.

He wasn’t sure of much, but knew with an uncertain certainty that somewhere in his future someone would be close to death, and the only way to save them would be to give his life in exchange.

Reliving days he had already relived added a certain mellowness to the air. Not knowing when his sacrifice was needed made every interaction a bit mind-numbing.

Quickly he realized that if his help was needed, he probably couldn’t die by random accidents. Walking in the streets late at night became somewhat of a delight to him, talking to the city - his only friend at this point.

In one of these walks, he saw a woman being followed. He knew his time had come once again. A million thoughts raced through his mind, mostly “I only walk through these streets cause I can’t be killed, somehow that deity knew that? Was I bound to walk these streets to find her?”

This time he didn’t have second thoughts; he saw the guy attempting to rape the girl. He jumped him, and the girl managed to run. But the man was having none of that, with a knife in his hand he slit Dan’s throat from ear to ear.

With the figure once again by his side, Dan laid there dying. The man escaped, but that was probably not his time, Dan thought as his brain was quickly shutting down. He was quickly led to see what had come out of his life, finishing with the last time his name was whispered.

Back at the bathroom. No sadness, no crying; only a bit of joy on having made a difference.

More sure than ever of his now purpose, he decided to embrace it.

Walking through those streets he knew he would be wherever he was needed, so he decided to travel the world. Sold all his belongings and bought a plane ticket to a random country. He knew his destiny was wherever the world took him.

Millions; hell billions, uncountable people had he saved. Never more than 3 months away from the incident.

His frail human mind couldn’t remember much from all these past lives - at this point he had lived for millennia at the same point in time - but that one interaction with the deity being was stuck in his head.

He knew he was being used. If anything, to him he was an Agent of Life, saving people through what he thought were parallel universes; to what purpose he wasn’t sure.

He was only really sure of one thing - he wasn’t free. Somehow, he would always end up where he was needed. His life was perfectly deterministic. Free-will more than ever became an illusion, something his mind accepted to not drive himself insane.

Still, all the sacrifices took a toll on him. At this point he knew there was no amount of knowledge that could save him, the outcome of all encounters would be his death.

Reset, to live in the same span of time. Disconnected from the world he was trying to save.

Seeing his family mourn his death an uncountable amount of times; seeing the suffering that he was powerless to fix.

Knowing that whatever love he found was bound to end.

Either way, he kept faithfully doing it, until something changed. At this point, it had been 6 months since the incident. In all the loops, it had never been more than 3. Something had definitely changed.

He wasn’t sure but something indeed felt different. For the first time in forever he felt free. The universe wasn’t bounding him.

Once again he felt something he had only felt in his teenage years. He had his entire life ahead of him. He could love and be loved. He could pass the time with the people he knew without being afraid.

The chemistry of his brain still wasn’t quite right, but he could only be hopeful for the future.

And so he went on with his life. He fell in love, and for a moment, if brief but powerful, he understood what it meant to be human.

By having children, he was again afraid for his own existence.

He was very careful to not mention of his past to no one. After all, no one would surely believe that such an insane thing had happened. That was his last personal sacrifice.

In his late 60s, he was still thinking daily about his past lives. Not that it mattered much at this point, but it was something he held on to. Very aware of the sacrifice he had made, and more than ever happy that he was able to do something with his life. Every waking moment a blessing.

When finally his heart beat its last. He fell to the ground by his lover, not sad; but glad that his moment had finally came. The being was once again there, to take him by the hand.

  • “Is this it? Is it finally over?”
  • “Yes, my friend. You’ve done good”
  • “I’m glad I was of service. Now tell me, before whatever is supposed to happen does, can you tell me what happened?”
  • “Haha, sure! I can’t help but love you humans, always curious even in the face of the complete unknown.”

“I can’t really tell you what I am as even I’m not so sure. Humans like to call me God, but as much as entropy is, I just am.

Time is an illusion that as much as it emanates from me, I have no control over it. You have no idea just how much exists because of it; all these many universes you’ve passed through.

I did what I had to do because as much as humans love to say I’m all powerful, I have no control over the physical world. It’s insane to have to watch over all, and being essentially powerless to stop the insanity that’s currently happening.

That’s where you come in, my dear.

Each individual person you saved is essential to the fate of that universe. Him or her dying would change entirely the outcome of this precisely crafted machine.

That’s the price of free-will. Humans are inherently deterministic, but for reasons not even I clearly understand you can never predict the result of their actions. You can add 3 humans to a room and suddenly you can’t ever predict what the hell the outcome is in 15 years.

        By sacrificing your own free-will you helped me make each of these universes more free, and for that I’m glad.

In the end I knew you were tired, and was sure to make you wake up in a universe where you could finish your life as a human for once.”

  • “I think I understand it, and I’m thankful for that second chance - even if it took that much of a sacrifice to end here. But tell me, how can you not know who you are?”
  • “You can’t possibly imagine just how much information I have acquired by watching the human race. It’s what I have done since the beginning of everything. But, to be honest, I spawned from nothing. There’s possibly something above me with a greater intent, but I can’t know for sure” - “Perhaps that is the nature of my existence, to make order out of chaos, but exactly why that is I don’t know”.

With that, I hope you’re free to go and become one with the universe.
